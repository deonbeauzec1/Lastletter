Last Letter

To those who have harmed me, I leave all my worldly forgiveness. For the harm they gave me was mine to bear, and I wish for it to be buried with me. I do not want to take any grudges with me either.

To my family I leave the words unspoken. The things you wish I would have said aloud. The things I did say, I only regret, for they fell on ears unwilling to listen. My bravery is extinguished. My fire is embers.

To those that I had the honour of loving. To you I leave responsibility. With the awareness of how selfish I am being. I ask the most—keep alive the memories we shared in your heart. Feed them and water them. Let them bloom into even better ones in new places with new people. Bring joy into this cruel world. Leave the pain, the fights and the sorrow with me. I can carry it away now. I truly believe I was built to be alone on this planet, but I resisted as long as I could. I am truly sorry. Do not mourn me. What I want for you is joy. And love. And a long, happy life.

—Deon
